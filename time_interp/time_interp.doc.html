module time_interp

     Version: v2.0
     Date:    October 5, 1999
     Contact: Bruce Wyman

OVERVIEW


     Computes variables for linearly interpolating between two dates.

     A time type is converted into two consecutive dates plus
     a fraction representing the distance between the dates.
     This information can be used to interpolate bewteen the dates.
     The dates may be expressed as years, months, or days.

OTHER MODULES USED


    time_manager_mod

PUBLIC INTERFACE


 use time_interp_mod [, only:  time_interp, fraction_of_year ]

   time_interp:       Determines variables for interpolating between
                      two dates.

   fraction_of_year:  Returns the fractional (0. to 1.) time of the year.

PUBLIC ROUTINES


call time_interp ( Time, frac, year1, year2 )
call time_interp ( Time, frac, year1, year2, month1, month2 )
call time_interp ( Time, frac, year1, year2, month1, month2, day1, day2 )

  Input

     Time    The time that the interpolation is computed for.
                [time_type]

  Output

     frac    The fractional (0. to 1.) location of argument Time
             between date1 and date2 (see notes below).
                [real]

     year1, month1, day1   The year, month, and day of the earlier date.
                              [integer]

     year2, month2, day2   The year, month, and day of the later date.
                              [integer]

  Notes:

    1) There are three variations of this interface.
       The dates must be expressed in the same manner; either as
       years, year and months, or years and months and days.

    2) To interpolate quantities (q1,q2) at date1 and date2, respectively,
       use the following:

                   q = q1 + frac * ( q2 - q1 )

--------------------------------------------------------------------

frac = fraction_of_year (Time)

  Input

     Time    The time (year) that the interpolation is computed for.
                [time_type]

  Returns

     The fractional (0. to 1.) amount into the year as determined by
     argument Time.  (Jan 1, 00z = 0.0 and Jan 1, 00z the next year = 1.0)
